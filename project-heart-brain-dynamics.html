Closed-Loop Modeling of Heart–Brain Dynamics

Master's Thesis • April 2025 – Present

Computational Neuroscience Dynamical Systems Multimodal Modeling Interoception
PDF

This project investigates bidirectional heart–brain interactions as a coupled dynamical system, with the goal of understanding how interoceptive signals shape neural dynamics and cognition. I modeled cortical regions and cardiac mechanisms as coupled Hopf oscillators, grounded in subject-specific structural connectivity.

I developed a generative Neural ODE framework that jointly predicts ECG and MEG signals from latent neural states, integrating mechanistic oscillator dynamics with learnable MLP-based observation models. Circuit-level dynamics are learned using Hebbian plasticity, with sequential inference of latent coupling parameters from multimodal neural data.

A key contribution of this work is the introduction of a closed-loop inference mechanism, where ECG reconstruction error is used to compute corrective feedback via an MLP that adapts cardiac oscillator states during inference. Multimodal preprocessing and source reconstruction were performed on the CAM-CAN MEG–ECG dataset using sLORETA to map sensor-space signals to 68 cortical parcels.
