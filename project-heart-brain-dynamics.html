Overview

Brain and heart as a coupled dynamical system

This project investigates bidirectional heart–brain interactions as a coupled dynamical system, with the goal of understanding how interoceptive signals shape neural dynamics and cognition. I modeled cortical regions and cardiac mechanisms as coupled Hopf oscillators, grounded in subject-specific structural connectivity from the CAM-CAN dataset.

Technical Approach

Generative Neural ODE framework

I developed a generative Neural ODE framework that jointly predicts ECG and MEG signals from latent neural states, integrating mechanistic oscillator dynamics with learnable MLP-based observation models. Circuit-level dynamics are learned using Hebbian plasticity, with sequential inference of latent coupling parameters from multimodal neural data.

  • Cortical + cardiac regions modeled as coupled Hopf oscillators
  • Neural ODE jointly predicts ECG + MEG from shared latent state
  • Hebbian plasticity for circuit-level coupling weight learning
  • sLORETA source reconstruction mapping MEG → 68 cortical parcels
  • Closed-loop inference: ECG error → corrective MLP feedback

A key contribution is the closed-loop inference mechanism: ECG reconstruction error drives a corrective MLP that adapts cardiac oscillator states during inference — making the model self-correcting in real time.

Dataset & Methods

CAM-CAN MEG–ECG, sLORETA source reconstruction

Multimodal preprocessing and source reconstruction were performed on the CAM-CAN MEG–ECG dataset using sLORETA to map sensor-space signals to 68 cortical parcels following the Desikan-Killiany atlas, enabling principled anatomical grounding for the dynamical model.
