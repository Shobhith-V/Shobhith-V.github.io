Problem & Motivation

Restoring proprioception for transfemoral amputees

Transfemoral amputees lack proprioceptive feedback from their missing limb, leading to impaired balance, gait awareness, and increased risk of falls. This project designed and evaluated a closed-loop sensory feedback system to restore proprioceptive cues during locomotion — improving balance, gait stability, and task performance in real-world walking scenarios.

Technical Approach

Arduino-based real-time closed-loop system

I implemented an Arduino-based real-time control system that translated ground reaction forces — measured via A301 force sensors — into vibrotactile feedback delivered through coin vibration motors. The closed-loop pipeline enabled intuitive, low-latency sensory substitution (<50ms) during locomotion.

In addition to vibrotactile feedback, I explored multimodal feedback strategies — including visual and auditory cues — and investigated different encoding schemes to enhance sensorimotor perception in lower-limb prosthetics. The system prioritized reliability under real-world, competitive conditions.

  • A301 force sensors → Arduino → coin motor vibrotactile actuators
  • Real-time ground reaction force encoding to vibration intensity/pattern
  • Cognitive load assessment using dual-task paradigm during trials
  • Multimodal feedback comparison: vibrotactile vs. visual vs. auditory

Results & Impact

The system's robustness was validated under high-stakes competitive conditions. An 8th-place global finish from a debut Indian team demonstrated that principled closed-loop design can match international standards in assistive neurotechnology.
