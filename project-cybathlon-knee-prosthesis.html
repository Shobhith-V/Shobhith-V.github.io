Active Knee Prosthesis Development - Cybathlon 2024

Competition • December 2023 - December 2024

Neuroprosthetics Embedded Systems Human–Machine Interaction Cognitive Neuroscience
Code • PDF

This project focused on the design and evaluation of a closed-loop sensory feedback system to restore proprioceptive cues for transfemoral amputees during locomotion. The goal was to improve balance, gait awareness, and task performance in real-world walking scenarios.

I implemented an Arduino-based real-time control system that translated ground reaction forces measured using A301 force sensors into vibrotactile feedback delivered through coin vibration motors. This closed-loop system enabled intuitive, low-latency sensory substitution during locomotion.

The effectiveness of the feedback was evaluated through task-based performance metrics inspired by Cybathlon challenges, along with cognitive load assessments to quantify usability and attentional demand. In addition to vibrotactile feedback, I explored multimodal feedback strategies—including visual and auditory cues—and investigated different encoding schemes to enhance sensorimotor perception in lower-limb prosthetics.

This work culminated in participation at Cybathlon 2024 in Zurich, an international competition that evaluates assistive technologies through realistic, everyday tasks performed by pilots with disabilities. Representing IIT Madras, our team achieved an 8th-place global finish and was the only Indian team at the event, validating the robustness and real-world performance of the system under competitive conditions.
