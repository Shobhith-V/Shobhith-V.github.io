Research Question

How does syntactic voice shape real-time reading?

This project investigates how syntactic voice constructions (active vs. passive) influence real-time sentence processing and attentional allocation. The central question: does passive voice increase cognitive load during incremental parsing, and does gaze behavior reveal the agent-first preference hypothesis?

Methodology

EyeLink Portable Duo + GAMMs

I designed and conducted a psycholinguistic eye-tracking experiment using PsychoPy on the EyeLink Portable Duo system to capture gaze dynamics during sentence comprehension. Stimuli were carefully constructed active/passive sentence pairs matched for length, frequency, and plausibility.

  • 48 participants — controlled for reading ability, native Telugu/Tamil speakers
  • EyeLink Portable Duo: 500Hz binocular gaze recording
  • PsychoPy for stimulus presentation and trigger synchronization
  • Generalized Additive Mixed Models (GAMMs) for time-resolved analysis
  • Nonlinear gaze trajectory modeling with subject-level random effects

GAMMs enabled flexible modeling of nonlinear fixation probability curves over time, capturing the moment-by-moment dynamics of how voice affects attention allocation — something traditional linear models would miss.

Conceptual Framework

Syntactic transformations as representational shifts

Conceptually, this work formalizes voice alternations as representational transformations over thematic role assignments, and examines how syntactic role assignment modulates attentional dynamics during incremental language comprehension. Results aim to validate or refine the agent-first parsing hypothesis in an Indian language context.
