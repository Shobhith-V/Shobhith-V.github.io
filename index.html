About

I am Lalanth Shobhith Vadlamudi, a final-year Dual Degree student at IIT Madras pursuing a B.Tech in Engineering Design and an M.Tech in Data Science.

My research focuses on understanding how the brain and body interact as a coupled dynamical system, with applications in neuroprosthetics, brain-computer interfaces, and computational models of cognition. I combine theoretical frameworks from dynamical systems theory with empirical neuroscience and modern machine learning to build principled models of neural computation.

I've had the opportunity to work on diverse projects - from developing closed-loop sensory feedback systems for lower-limb prosthetics (Cybathlon 2024) to modeling heart-brain dynamics using Neural ODEs, and applying deep learning to bioprocess optimization at Pfizer. Each project deepens my understanding of how biological systems process information and adapt in real-time.

Featured Achievement

Team Asai at Cybathlon 2024

Representing IIT Madras at Cybathlon 2024 in Zurich, our team achieved 8th place globally in the Powered Leg Prosthesis Race - the only Indian team at the event. I developed the signal processing and closed-loop sensory feedback system that enabled real-time proprioceptive cues through vibrotactile feedback, improving balance and gait awareness for transfemoral amputees.
