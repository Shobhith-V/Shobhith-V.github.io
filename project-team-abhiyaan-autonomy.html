Context

IIT Madras' autonomous ground vehicle team

Team Abhiyaan is IIT Madras' student team developing autonomous ground vehicles for national and international competitions. As part of the team, I worked on the drive-by-wire control systems — the hardware/software interface that enables a commercial golf cart to be fully controlled autonomously.

Technical Work

Brake-by-wire and steer-by-wire systems

I designed and implemented the brake-by-wire and steer-by-wire systems for the vehicle platform — replacing mechanical linkages with electronically controlled actuators that the autonomous stack could command in real time.

  • Brake-by-wire: linear actuator + encoder feedback for precise braking force control
  • Steer-by-wire: DC servo motor + angle feedback for autonomous steering
  • CAN bus integration with the ROS-based perception and planning stack
  • Safety interlocks: watchdog timers, fault detection, and manual override circuits
  • Latency characterization and tuning for real-time control performance

The system enabled the full autonomous navigation pipeline — from LiDAR-based perception through path planning to vehicle actuation — to operate reliably under outdoor competition conditions.

Impact

1st place at IGVC Design Challenge, Michigan

The team placed 1st among 25+ global universities in the Design Challenge at the Intelligent Ground Vehicle Competition (IGVC) at Oakland University, Michigan. I also presented the team's engineering and societal impact to delegates from the education ministries of G20 countries — a recognition of the project's broader significance beyond competition performance.
